# Purpose

This document defines the mathematical framework that powers every intelligence engine inside SkillMint.

Unlike AI-001, which defines the architecture of the intelligence platform, this document defines how intelligence is measured.

Every score, recommendation and career decision must follow the rules described here.

This document is the mathematical source of truth for SkillMint.

---

# Philosophy

SkillMint does not measure resumes.

SkillMint measures careers.

A resume is only evidence.

Career Intelligence is the interpretation of that evidence.

Every score generated by SkillMint should answer one question:

> "How likely is this person to succeed in achieving their next career objective?"

The objective is not perfection.

The objective is measurable progress.

---

# Intelligence Principles

Every intelligence engine must satisfy seven permanent principles.

## Principle 1 — Objectivity

Scores should originate from measurable evidence.

Personal opinions should never influence deterministic calculations.

---

## Principle 2 — Explainability

Every score must explain:

- What increased it
- What decreased it
- Why it matters
- What should improve next

No unexplained numbers should exist anywhere in SkillMint.

---

## Principle 3 — Consistency

Identical inputs must always generate identical outputs.

Randomness is prohibited inside deterministic scoring.

---

## Principle 4 — Comparability

Scores should remain comparable over time.

A Career IQ of 82 today should represent approximately the same level of career strength as an 82 six months later.

Algorithm improvements should create new scoring versions rather than rewriting history.

---

## Principle 5 — Independence

Every intelligence engine owns exactly one responsibility.

Career IQ should never secretly modify Recruiter Confidence.

Career Risk should never secretly modify Salary Intelligence.

Engines exchange evidence—not scores.

---

## Principle 6 — Actionability

Every weakness must produce an improvement opportunity.

If SkillMint identifies a problem, it must recommend a realistic solution.

Diagnosis without guidance has little value.

---

## Principle 7 — Growth

SkillMint rewards improvement rather than perfection.

A user improving rapidly should receive visible recognition even before reaching elite career scores.

Long-term progress is more important than short-term rankings.

# Universal Scoring Framework

Every intelligence engine follows the same structure.

```text
Evidence

↓

Feature Extraction

↓

Deterministic Calculation

↓

Raw Score

↓

Normalization

↓

Final Score (0–100)

↓

AI Explanation

↓

Recommendation
```

Each stage has one responsibility.

Stages should never be skipped.

| 60–69 | Developing |

# Score Components

Every score consists of four independent components.

## Raw Score

Direct mathematical output before normalization.

---

## Normalized Score

Converted into a consistent 0–100 scale.

---

## Confidence

Measures confidence in the evaluation itself.

Confidence is **not** the same as score.

A user may receive:

Career IQ = 82

Confidence = 58%

This indicates that additional evidence could improve the accuracy of the assessment.

---

## Evidence Quality

Evidence Quality measures the completeness of available career information.

Factors include:

- Resume completeness
- Profile completeness
- Project evidence
- Certification evidence
- Employment history
- Portfolio quality

Poor evidence reduces confidence—not necessarily score.

---

# Career IQ

Career IQ is SkillMint's flagship metric.

It represents the overall health of a user's professional profile.

Career IQ does **not** estimate intelligence.

Career IQ does **not** estimate future success.

Career IQ estimates current career readiness based on measurable evidence.

It should answer one question:

> "If a recruiter evaluated this candidate today, how prepared are they for their next career opportunity?"

Career IQ becomes the foundation for every other intelligence engine.

---

# Career IQ Components

Career IQ is composed of eight dimensions.

| Dimension | Purpose |
|------------|---------|
| Profile Completeness | Measures missing career information |
| Experience Strength | Evaluates professional experience |
| Skills Quality | Evaluates technical and professional skills |
| Project Portfolio | Measures practical evidence of ability |
| Education | Evaluates educational foundation |
| Certifications | Measures verified learning |
| Career Progression | Measures growth over time |
| Professional Credibility | Measures trust signals and consistency |

No single dimension should dominate Career IQ.

The objective is balanced career evaluation.

---

|-----------|--------:|

Weights may evolve in future scoring versions, but historical assessments must always retain the weights used at the time they were generated.

---

# Career IQ Interpretation

Career IQ is designed to answer:

- How complete is this professional profile?
- How credible is the candidate?
- How prepared is the user for their next opportunity?
- Where should the user invest effort next?

Career IQ should never be interpreted as an absolute measure of talent.

It is a dynamic indicator of career readiness.

# ATS Compatibility

ATS Compatibility measures how effectively a resume can be interpreted by Applicant Tracking Systems.

It is **not** a measure of candidate quality.

It measures document quality.

A highly qualified candidate can still receive a poor ATS score.

Likewise, a perfectly ATS-optimized resume does not imply strong employability.

ATS Compatibility answers one question:

> "Can modern Applicant Tracking Systems correctly understand this resume?"

---

# ATS Components

| Component | Weight |
|-----------|-------:|
| Resume Structure | 20% |
| Section Organization | 15% |
| Keyword Coverage | 25% |
| Formatting Simplicity | 15% |
| Contact Information | 5% |
| Skills Representation | 10% |
| Experience Formatting | 10% |

Total = 100%

---

# ATS Principles

ATS should reward:

- Clear hierarchy
- Standard headings
- Relevant keywords
- Readable formatting
- Machine-readable structure

ATS should penalize:

- Tables
- Excessive graphics
- Missing headings
- Poor keyword usage
- Hidden text
- Decorative layouts

| 80–89 | Strong |
| 70–79 | Acceptable |
| 60–69 | Risky |
| Below 60 | High probability of parsing issues |

# Recruiter Confidence

Recruiter Confidence estimates how likely an experienced recruiter is to continue evaluating the candidate after an initial profile review.

Unlike ATS Compatibility, this score models human judgement.

It answers one question:

> "Would a recruiter invest additional time evaluating this candidate?"

Recruiter Confidence should never depend on subjective opinions.

Every increase or decrease must originate from measurable evidence.

---

# Recruiter Confidence Components

| Component | Weight |
|-----------|-------:|
| Experience Credibility | 20% |
| Project Quality | 20% |
| Skill Relevance | 15% |
| Career Consistency | 10% |
| Achievement Evidence | 10% |
| Resume Presentation | 10% |
| Professional Branding | 5% |
| Education Relevance | 5% |
| Certification Value | 5% |

Total = 100%

---

# Recruiter Confidence Penalties

The following reduce confidence significantly:

- Large unexplained employment gaps
- Inconsistent timelines
- Generic project descriptions
- Keyword stuffing
- Unsupported claims
- Duplicate skills
- Unrealistic achievements
- Missing measurable outcomes

Penalties reduce confidence rather than permanently limiting Career IQ.

| Below 60 | Significant recruiter concerns |

# Career DNA

Career DNA is **not** another score.

Career DNA is a behavioural profile.

Its purpose is to personalize recommendations rather than rank candidates.

Career DNA answers one question:

> "How does this person naturally build their career?"

Career DNA should never be interpreted as personality testing.

Instead, it describes observable professional patterns.

---

# Career DNA Dimensions

Career DNA is composed of six dimensions.

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Builder | Creates projects and products |
| Specialist | Develops deep expertise |
| Explorer | Learns across multiple domains |
| Leader | Organizes and influences teams |
| Executor | Delivers consistent outcomes |
| Innovator | Demonstrates creativity and experimentation |

Every user possesses all six dimensions.

Career DNA measures their relative strength rather than assigning rigid categories.

---

# Career DNA Evidence

Career DNA is inferred from:

- Projects
- Skills
- Work experience
- Career progression
- Certifications
- Portfolio
- Contributions
- Learning behaviour

Career DNA should improve continuously as new evidence becomes available.

---

# Career DNA Usage

Career DNA influences:

- Mission selection
- Recommendation ranking
- Learning paths
- Career roadmap generation
- Dashboard personalization

Career DNA must never influence Career IQ.

It personalizes recommendations.

It does not judge capability.

# Career Momentum

Career Momentum measures the direction and speed of a user's career growth.

Unlike Career IQ, which represents the current state, Career Momentum measures change over time.

It answers one question:

> "Is this person's career improving, stagnating or declining?"

Momentum rewards consistent progress rather than absolute achievement.

A beginner making rapid progress should have higher Momentum than an experienced professional whose profile has remained unchanged for months.

---

# Career Momentum Components

| Component | Weight |
|-----------|-------:|
| Skill Growth | 25% |
| Project Activity | 20% |
| Resume Improvement | 15% |
| Learning Progress | 15% |
| Recommendation Completion | 15% |
| Career Consistency | 10% |

Total = 100%

---

# Momentum Events

Positive events include:

- New verified skills
- Stronger projects
- Resume improvements
- Mission completion
- Portfolio updates
- Certifications
- Consistent learning

Negative events include:

- Long inactivity
- Ignored recommendations
- Stale profile
- Declining portfolio quality

Momentum should never decrease because another user performs better.

Momentum is always personal.

---

# Momentum Interpretation

| Score | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 90–100 | Exceptional growth |
| 80–89 | Strong momentum |
| 70–79 | Healthy progress |
| 60–69 | Slow progress |
| Below 60 | Stagnation detected |

# Career Risk

Career Risk measures the probability that the user's current career trajectory will reduce future opportunities.

It is an early-warning system.

Career Risk answers one question:

> "What could realistically prevent this user from achieving their next career objective?"

Unlike Career IQ, lower Career Risk is desirable.

---

# Career Risk Components

| Component | Weight |
|-----------|-------:|
| Skill Gap | 25% |
| Experience Gap | 20% |
| Resume Quality Issues | 15% |
| Portfolio Weakness | 15% |
| Market Competitiveness | 15% |
| Career Inactivity | 10% |

Total = 100%

---

# Risk Categories

Instead of only providing numbers, SkillMint classifies Career Risk into actionable categories.

| Risk | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Critical | Immediate intervention required |
| High | Serious weaknesses affecting employability |
| Moderate | Noticeable improvement required |
| Low | Healthy career trajectory |
| Minimal | No significant concerns detected |

Users should remember their category more than the numerical score.

---

# Risk Philosophy

Career Risk should never punish users.

Instead, it highlights the highest-value problems.

Every identified risk must produce at least one recommendation.

Risk without action is useless.

---

# Career Forecast

Career Forecast replaces the original "Career Potential" concept.

Potential implies certainty.

Forecast communicates probability.

Career Forecast predicts the likely career outcome if the user continues their current trajectory.

It answers one question:

> "If nothing changes, where is this career likely to be in the future?"

Forecasts are probabilistic.

They are not guarantees.

---

# Forecast Inputs

Career Forecast considers:

- Career IQ
- Career Momentum
- Career Risk
- Recommendation completion
- Market demand
- Learning behaviour
- Historical improvement

The system should explicitly state that forecasts become more reliable as additional evidence becomes available.

---

# Forecast Horizon

SkillMint initially supports:

- 6-month forecast
- 12-month forecast
- 24-month forecast

Longer predictions become increasingly uncertain.

Confidence should decrease accordingly.

---

# Forecast Output

Instead of returning a score, Career Forecast returns structured outcomes.

Example:

Career Outlook

Positive

Probability

82%

Primary Drivers

- Strong momentum
- High-demand skills
- Consistent learning

Primary Risks

- Limited professional experience
- Weak project diversity

Highest ROI Action

Build one production-level project within the next 30 days.

This format is significantly more useful than a numerical score.

# Decision Engine

The Decision Engine is the central reasoning system of SkillMint.

It converts intelligence into action.

Every recommendation shown to a user must originate from the Decision Engine.

The Decision Engine answers one question:

> "What is the single highest-value action this user should take next?"

Recommendations should never be random.

They should always maximize measurable career improvement.

---

# Recommendation Priority Framework

Every recommendation receives a Decision Score.

The Decision Score determines recommendation order.

The Decision Score considers five dimensions.

| Dimension | Weight |
|-----------|-------:|
| Career Impact | 35% |
| Urgency | 25% |
| Effort Required | 15% |
| Evidence Confidence | 15% |
| Completion Probability | 10% |

Total = 100%

Recommendations are always sorted by Decision Score.

---

# Career Impact

Career Impact measures how much the recommendation is expected to improve the user's long-term employability.

Examples:

High Impact

- Build production project
- Gain internship
- Improve resume structure

Medium Impact

- Complete certification
- Improve LinkedIn

Low Impact

- Minor formatting improvements

---

# Urgency

Urgency measures how quickly the recommendation should be completed.

Examples:

Critical

High

Medium

Low

Future

Urgency changes dynamically.

---

# Effort Required

Recommendations should communicate expected effort.

Categories:

Quick Win

Small Project

Medium Project

Large Project

Long-Term Goal

Users should understand the investment before starting.

---

# Completion Probability

SkillMint estimates the probability that the user can successfully complete the recommendation.

Factors include:

- Current skills

- Available evidence

- Previous completion history

- Career DNA

Recommendations should stretch the user—but remain realistic.

# Confidence Framework

Every intelligence engine reports confidence independently.

Confidence is determined by evidence quality—not score quality.

Example:

Career IQ = 86

Confidence = 92%

Another user may receive:

Career IQ = 86

Confidence = 61%

The score remains identical.

The certainty differs.

---

# Confidence Levels

| Confidence | Interpretation |
|------------|----------------|
| 90–100 | Very High |
| 80–89 | High |
| 70–79 | Moderate |
| 60–69 | Limited |
| Below 60 | Insufficient Evidence |

Low confidence should encourage users to improve their profile—not reduce their score.

---

# Scoring Versioning

Every Career Assessment stores:

- Assessment Version
- Scoring Version
- Intelligence Version
- Recommendation Version

Historical assessments are immutable.

Algorithm improvements must never rewrite previous results.

Users should always be able to compare historical assessments fairly.

---

# Validation Framework

Every intelligence engine must satisfy four validation criteria.

## Mathematical Validation

Calculations produce deterministic outputs.

---

## Business Validation

Scores accurately represent career evidence.

---

## User Validation

Users understand why scores were generated.

---

## Outcome Validation

Following recommendations should improve future assessments.

If recommendations fail to improve user outcomes over time, the intelligence model should be reviewed.

---

# Success Criteria

The Career Intelligence Framework is successful when:

- Every score is explainable.
- Every recommendation is evidence-based.
- Every recommendation is actionable.
- Historical assessments remain comparable.
- Users understand what to improve next.
- Completing recommendations measurably improves future career assessments.

---

# Final Principle

SkillMint does not optimize for high scores.

SkillMint optimizes for better careers.

Scores are measurements.

Recommendations are actions.

Career growth is the outcome.

The success of SkillMint is not determined by the intelligence it generates, but by the measurable improvement it creates in people's careers.

# Purpose

This document defines the philosophy, objectives and operating principles of the SkillMint Intelligence Engine.

It does **not** define mathematical formulas.

Those are defined in subsequent AI documents.

This document defines **how SkillMint thinks**.

---

# Mission

SkillMint exists to answer one question:

> **What is the single highest-impact action this person can take today to improve their career?**

Everything the Intelligence Engine produces must support this mission.

---

# Guiding Principles

## Principle 1 — Radical Honesty

SkillMint never inflates scores.

Scores reflect how the user's profile is likely to be evaluated in the real hiring market.

SkillMint measures market readiness—not personal worth.

---

## Principle 2 — Actionability

Every weakness must produce a recommendation.

Every recommendation must produce an expected outcome.

Every outcome must be measurable.

Users are never left wondering what to do next.

---

## Principle 3 — Explainability

Every score must be explainable.

Users should always be able to answer:

* Why is my score this value?
* Which factors influenced it?
* Which factor hurts me the most?
* What is the fastest way to improve?

The Intelligence Engine must never behave like a black box.

---

## Principle 4 — Evidence Over Claims

Claims without supporting evidence receive little or no credit.

Examples:

* "Strong leadership skills" without examples has low value.
* "Led a 5-member team that reduced deployment time by 32%" has high value.

The engine rewards demonstrated impact rather than descriptive language.

---

## Principle 5 — Market Alignment

Scoring adapts to hiring trends.

The same resume may receive different recommendations depending on:

* Role
* Industry
* Location
* Seniority
* Current market demand

---

## Principle 6 — Progress Over Perfection

Users are evaluated against both:

1. Current market expectations.
2. Their own historical progress.

Improvement is rewarded, but it never replaces objective readiness.

# Core Intelligence Engines

The Intelligence Engine is composed of the following systems:

* Career IQ Engine
* ATS Engine
* Recruiter Confidence Engine
* Salary Intelligence Engine
* Career DNA Engine
* Recommendation Engine
* Mission Generator
* Achievement Engine

Each engine has an independent responsibility but shares a common candidate profile.

---

# Design Goals

The engine should:

* Be transparent.
* Be consistent.
* Be difficult to manipulate.
* Encourage genuine improvement.
* Reward measurable achievements.
* Prioritize quality over quantity.

---

# Anti-Principles

The engine must never:

* Inflate scores to make users feel good.
* Penalize users for factors outside their control.
* Reward keyword stuffing.
* Reward fabricated achievements.
* Encourage dishonest resumes.
* Hide the reasoning behind recommendations.

---

# Output Requirements

Every analysis produced by SkillMint must include:

1. A score.
2. Confidence level.
3. Explanation.
4. Strengths.
5. Weaknesses.
6. Highest-impact recommendation.
7. Estimated improvement if completed.

Every output should guide the next decision rather than simply report the current state.

---

# Success Definition

The Intelligence Engine succeeds when users can confidently answer:

* Where do I stand today?
* Why am I here?
* What should I improve first?
* How much will it help?
* How do I measure success?

The Intelligence Engine exists to transform uncertainty into clarity.

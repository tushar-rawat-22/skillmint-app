# 000 — The SkillMint Constitution

**Version:** 1.0

**Status:** Frozen

---

# Purpose

This document defines the permanent principles upon which SkillMint is built.

It is not a product specification.

It is not a technical document.

It is the constitution of the company.

Every future decision—product, engineering, design, AI, hiring, marketing, pricing, and partnerships—must align with this document.

If a future decision conflicts with this constitution, the constitution takes precedence.

---

# Why SkillMint Exists

People do not fail because they lack potential.

They fail because they lack clarity.

Modern careers are filled with uncertainty:

* What skills should I learn?
* Why am I getting rejected?
* Am I underpaid?
* What should I improve first?
* What should I do next?

Existing tools answer fragments of these questions.

SkillMint exists to answer them together.

Our mission is to transform uncertainty into clarity.

---

# Vision

To become the world's most trusted Career Operating System.

Every professional should know:

* Where they stand today.
* Where they can go.
* What is holding them back.
* What they should do next.

---

# Mission

Help people make better career decisions through honest, explainable intelligence.

We do not promise jobs.

We promise better decisions.

---

# What SkillMint Is

SkillMint is a Career Operating System.

It continuously analyzes a person's professional profile, identifies opportunities, measures progress, and recommends the highest-impact next action.

The resume is only the starting point.

---

# What SkillMint Is Not

SkillMint is not:

* a resume builder
* an ATS keyword generator
* a generic chatbot
* a motivational platform
* a course marketplace
* a job board

Those products solve isolated problems.

SkillMint solves career progression.

---

# Our Customer

We build for ambitious professionals who want objective feedback and measurable growth.

Our users value truth over compliments.

They want clarity over motivation.

They are willing to improve when they understand why improvement matters.

---

# Core Values

## Radical Honesty

We never inflate scores.

We never hide weaknesses.

We never optimize for temporary satisfaction.

Trust is earned through truth.

---

## Explain Everything

Every recommendation.

Every score.

Every prediction.

Every insight.

Must be explainable.

If the user cannot understand why something happened, the system has failed.

---

## Progress Over Perfection

SkillMint rewards improvement.

People are compared against professional standards and against their own historical progress.

Growth matters.

---

## Evidence Over Claims

Claims without evidence receive little credit.

Demonstrated achievements are more valuable than descriptive language.

---

## Simplicity

Complexity is hidden behind thoughtful design.

Users experience clarity, not algorithms.

---

# Product Principles

Every feature must satisfy all of the following:

1. Increase career clarity.
2. Improve hiring probability.
3. Encourage measurable growth.
4. Be explainable.
5. Support long-term retention.

If it fails any one principle, it does not belong in SkillMint.

---

# Engineering Philosophy

Technology exists to serve the product.

The product exists to serve the user.

We optimize for maintainability, scalability, and long-term clarity.

No engineering shortcut is worth sacrificing trust.

---

# AI Philosophy

Artificial Intelligence should augment judgment—not replace it.

The Intelligence Engine assists users in making better decisions.

Final decisions remain human.

---

# Success Definition

SkillMint succeeds when users can confidently answer:

* Where do I stand?
* Why am I here?
* What should I improve?
* What should I do next?
* How do I measure success?

---

# Long-Term Vision

SkillMint begins with resumes.

It evolves into lifelong career intelligence.

Over time, SkillMint should understand:

* resumes
* portfolios
* LinkedIn
* GitHub
* certifications
* interviews
* salaries
* career goals
* professional growth

Every interaction contributes to a continuously improving understanding of the user's career.

---

# Final Principle

Everything we build should increase the user's confidence—not because we make them feel good, but because we help them see reality clearly and act on it intelligently.

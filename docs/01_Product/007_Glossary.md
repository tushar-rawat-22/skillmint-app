# Purpose

Defines official terminology used throughout the SkillMint ecosystem.

These definitions are authoritative.

---

## SkillMint

An AI Career Operating System designed to help users understand, improve, and accelerate their careers.

---

## Career Workspace

The primary dashboard where users interact with analysis, recommendations, progress, and career insights.

---

## Career IQ

SkillMint's proprietary employability index.

Measures overall career readiness using multiple weighted factors.

---

## ATS Score

A measurement of resume compatibility with Applicant Tracking Systems.

---

## Recruiter Confidence

An estimate of how likely a recruiter is to consider the resume for further evaluation.

---

## Salary Intelligence

An estimate of salary range based on skills, experience, education, market trends, and resume quality.

---

## Skills Radar

A visual representation of strengths and skill gaps.

---

## Resume Analysis

The complete AI-assisted evaluation of a user's resume.

---

## Career Roadmap

A personalized sequence of recommendations designed to improve employability.

---

## Personalized Recommendation

An AI-generated action prioritized according to expected career impact.

---

## Resume Parser

The system responsible for converting uploaded resumes into structured information.

---

## Employability

A user's readiness for desired employment opportunities.

---

## Career Milestone

A measurable achievement within a user's long-term career journey.

---

## Weekly Progress

The measurable improvement achieved over a rolling seven-day period.

---

## MVP

Minimum Viable Product.

The smallest publicly usable version of SkillMint capable of delivering core value.

---

## Career Operating System (Career OS)

The long-term vision for SkillMint.

A unified platform that assists users throughout every stage of their professional journey.

---

# Naming Convention

Throughout all documentation:

- "Dashboard" refers to the page.
- "Career Workspace" refers to the product module.
- "Career IQ" always refers to the proprietary employability index.
- "AI" always provides recommendations, not decisions.

These naming conventions must remain consistent across engineering, product, design, marketing, and documentation.
